Portalcraft ネメシス (Nemesis) Tier 1

Redraws: 2 · Difficulty: Normal (inferred)

Game plan. Pure midrange: build boards, then grind the last points of damage. Artifact-variety bonuses are nice but optional — take AF cards mainly for the resource value.

Early

Curve out with followers. Isaac, Congenial Engineerアイザック and チルスケーターチルスケーター stack Artifact variety; Stone Breakerストーンブレイク or Marionette Masterステージメーカー all but locks the early game.

Mid (decisive)

Land evolve-effect followers: エッジーマスターエッジーマスター then Myriad Designs凡百の製図 on consecutive turns wins roughly 8 games in 10 per Whalt. Prioritise board over AF-variety farming until turn 6.

Late

Standard midrange maths — push face when lethal is in range, otherwise keep trading. スカーレットスカーレット and Chaos Legionケイオスレギオン deal 6 while removing without spending evolves, perfect for split lethal.

The class in one line

Board power and reach at once; weak in removal and draw, so lean forward and force the opponent to have answers.

Abysscraft ナイトメア (Nightmare) Tier 1

Redraws: 1 · Difficulty: Easy-Normal (inferred)

Game plan. Was the runaway best class; post-nerf the low-rarity midrange plan is the reliable line, with Belial, Archangel of Cunningベリアル or Armes, Depletive Demonアルメス as premium finisher plans when offered.

Early

Strong 2-drops (Almeida, Headstrong Minerアルメイダ, Support Wolfリリーブウルフ with Enhance) mean you can hard-mulligan for タイトロープキャットタイトロープキャット or golds and still hit your curve.

Mid (decisive)

Stake the game on turns 4-6: タイトロープキャットタイトロープキャット is the class's best card; Fickle Necromancerトラブルネクロマンサー and Crimson Soulmancer紅札の魂魄使い also build wide boards. Losing the mid game wrecks every win plan.

Late

Push your chosen line: chip damage from the air, or use Allure of the Mightiest「最強」の魅惑 to win the evolve-point war and take the board back.

The class in one line

Highest average card power — almost no dead low-rarity picks. Only 1 redraw, so expect fewer Golds/Legendaries; play aggressively rather than waiting for a perfect curve.

Swordcraft ロイヤル (Royal) Tier 2

Redraws: 2 · Difficulty: Easy (inferred)

Game plan. Midrange that can lean aggro or control. Best early-game statlines in the mode — never pass a turn.

Early

Strongest phase. Curve followers every turn; if the opponent stumbles once you can snowball to a win. Expect kept removal (Stone Breakerストーンブレイク, 夜の唱のライブ夜の唱のライブ) from second players — treat an early blowout as a bonus, not the plan.

Mid

斬奏の衛生兵斬奏の衛生兵 is the engine — strong enough to keep alone in mulligans; fire it by turn 6 even if cards overflow. No medic? Any evolve-effect follower holds the fort; if draw stalls, force boards so the opponent cannot sit back either.

Late

Few long-game cards (Golden Knight, True King's Blade黄金の騎士 Golden Knight, シエテ) — if you drafted few of them, pick a moment to take a calculated all-in. セザールセザール is the class's #1 card: an on-curve play that flips losing boards and wins winning ones, worth considering Royal for on sight.

The class in one line

Tempo and resources simultaneously — trade endlessly while the medic and gold-coin draws keep the hand full.

Redraws: 8 · Difficulty: Very hard (inferred)

Game plan. Weakest class even at 8 redraws (Whalt's own assessment — he admits no reliable line). Aim a reactive control deck at exactly 20 damage from Lyanthoth, Eld Tomeリアントース, Storm bodies and burn.

Early

Set amulets and accept tempo loss; clear only what you must. Prostrating Coward崇奉の怯者 and Scripture of Salvation救済の聖典 claw life back later.

Mid

Time plays to amulet pops. AoE (Desperate Shrinemouse窮鼠の斎女, ブロードミッショナリーブロードミッショナリー) resets strong boards; Saint of Rehabilitation威徳の至聖, Sara, Graphos's Chosenサラ, Sophia, Zeyen Priestessソフィア build from amulets; Pegasus Riderペガサスの乗り手 develops and hits face at once. Late

Games average 12+ turns. Attacking line: Pegasus Riderペガサスの乗り手 + 伝達の聖鳥伝達の聖鳥 for repeated face damage. Defensive line: heal with イニシアイニシア / 伝達の聖鳥伝達の聖鳥, pressure with Vira, Luminous Primal Knightヴィーラ and wait for their hand to fail; Lyanthoth, Eld Tomeリアントース faith is the slow-lane finisher.

The class in one line

When short on damage you are long on removal and healing — against damage-poor midrange decks the pure control win is real. Otherwise heavily matchup-dependent.

Drafting

  1. Pick the class from the Golds/Legendaries shown, not rank alone. A Tier 1 class with weak opening picks starts behind. Exception per Whalt at set launch: take Nightmare-tier classes unless both shown cards are near-unplayable.
  2. Picks 1-8: take raw power. Choose the stronger pair; if unsure, score all four cards and take the higher total.
  3. Picks 9-13: fix the curve. 5-6 cards at 2-3 cost is the floor — passing early turns against the midrange trio is fatal. Skip strong-but-expensive picks once the top of the curve is full.
  4. Draft roles, not just stats. Cover removal, AoE, healing, reach and board refill so no game state locks you out.
  5. Redraw on weak pairs. A redraw replaces all four cards and can roll Golds/Legendaries. With 5-8 redraws (Witch/Elf/Bishop), redraw aggressively towards your archetype; with 1-2, spend them only on genuinely bad pairs.
  6. Last Gold/Legendary completes the deck. Take what your plan is missing, not automatically the highest-rated card.

Playing

  1. Win the mid game. Turns 4-6 decide this environment. Plan mulligans and evolves around your best turn-4-6 sequence.
  2. Know your deck. Curve, win condition, removal count, ideal opener — it drives every mulligan and tempo call.
  3. Board control is everything. Removal is scarce; followers stick. Hold AoE until the opponent commits.
  4. Bank evolution points. Few finishers means long games; late finishers usually need evolves. Force their EP spend while conserving yours.
  5. Read unfamiliar cards. The pool includes 2Pick-only Bronzes/Silvers; check effects as they appear.
  6. Read their picks. A weak card on their board means its draft partner was strong — expect the pair-mate.
